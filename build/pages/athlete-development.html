Step 1:
Initial Consultation

Before we train, we listen.

Every athlete is different. We ask the right questions — about level, strengths, gaps, goals, and how serious you are about getting there. This is where we recommend the right program (private, small group, or elite) and set clear expectations. By the end, you know exactly what to expect, and we know exactly what we're building.

Step 2:
Player Evaluation

Assessment meets action on day one.

Your first training session isn't a light trial — it's real training. Coach Gino evaluates you across ball-handling, footwork, shooting mechanics, decision-making, athleticism, and mindset. This isn't a free "trial assessment." You're training, working, and being coached — the evaluation is embedded in the work. By the end, Coach has a clear picture of where you are and what you need.

Step 3:
Personalized Development Plan

Strategy before repetition.

After the evaluation, we build a development roadmap that's yours alone. It names the 2–3 critical skill gaps to address first, the priority drills, how often you need to train, and the milestones we'll hit along the way. The plan isn't vague — it's specific. Every athlete knows what they're training for and why.

Step 4:
Weekly Skill Development

Consistency compounds.

Every session follows the plan — no random drills. Each rep is designed: shooting footwork, dribble moves under fatigue, decision-making under pressure, strength and conditioning. The magic happens over weeks. Skills that feel awkward in week one become second nature by week four. Confidence builds because you know you're getting better every session.

Step 5:
Progress Reviews

Measure. Celebrate. Adjust.

We don't guess if you're improving — we measure it. Every few weeks we pause and assess progress against the plan. Is the footwork crisp? Is your shot more consistent? Are you making smarter decisions in games? We celebrate the wins and adjust: if a priority isn't landing, we shift tactics; if you've crushed a milestone, we raise the bar.

Step 6:
Continued Advancement

Mastery opens doors.

There's no finish line in basketball. Once you've mastered a skill, the next one is waiting. Once you've crushed a goal, a harder one takes its place. We keep evolving the plan — adding complexity, raising the competition level, building for the next chapter. You're not in a cycle. You're on a trajectory.
